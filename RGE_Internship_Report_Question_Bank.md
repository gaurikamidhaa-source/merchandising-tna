# Richa Global Exports — Internship Report Question Bank

**Purpose:** Department-wise questions to ask heads/experts at RGE so your report is built on real, primary data — not assumptions or copied structure from your senior's Brandix report.

---

## Before You Start — Ground Rules for Authenticity

- **Note who, when, what role** for every answer (e.g. "Mr. X, Merchandising Manager, 14 Jul"). This is what makes a report defensible in viva and distinguishes it from a copy-paste job.
- **Ask for the document, not just the description** wherever possible — org chart, TNA template, AQL chart, compliance checklist, process flow. A photographed or screenshotted real document >> your own redrawn version.
- **Ask to physically walk the floor** for Sampling, Cutting, Production, QA, and Finishing — what you see will generate better follow-up questions than any list.
- **Keep each session to 15–20 min**, 6–8 questions max per sitting — department heads are busy; you can always go back for a follow-up.
- **Cross-check numbers** (headcount, capacity, lead time) with two people if you can — export house data often varies between what's "on paper" and what's "on the floor."
- Skip any section/department that genuinely doesn't exist at your unit — don't force RGE into Brandix's mold.

## 1. Company / Unit Overview
*Ask: Unit Head / HR / your Industry Mentor*

1. What's the ownership and group structure of Richa Global Exports — standalone or part of a larger group?
2. How many manufacturing/processing units does RGE operate, and which one(s) is this internship based at?
3. What's the year of establishment, and how has the buyer portfolio evolved since then?
4. What are the current key buyers/clients (you already know Talbots, Lilly Pulitzer, Tommy Bahama, Johnny Was — ask if there are others, and which is the largest by volume/value)?
5. What's the total headcount at this unit, and the rough split between merchandising, production, and support functions?
6. What product categories/garment types does this unit specialize in (e.g. knits vs. wovens, dresses, sportswear)?
7. Is there a parent/sister unit structure — does RGE outsource cutting/sewing to job-work vendors, or is everything in-house?
8. What certifications does the unit hold (WRAP, SEDEX/SMETA, GOTS, OCS, ISO, etc.)?
9. Could I get a copy of the organisational chart for the unit?

---

## 2. Merchandising Department *(your core department — go deepest here)*
*Ask: your Industry Mentor / Senior Merchandiser / Merchandising Manager*

**Structure & roles**
1. What does the merchandising team's hierarchy look like (Merchandising Manager → Sr. Merchandiser → Merchandiser → Merchandising Coordinator, etc.)?
2. How are merchandisers allocated — by buyer, by product category, or by both?
3. How many active buyers/styles does one merchandiser typically handle at a time?

**Order lifecycle**
4. Walk me through the order lifecycle from buyer inquiry to shipment — what are the key milestones?
5. How is a Time and Action (TNA) calendar built for a new order — which department gives which date, and who owns the master calendar?
6. What's the standard lead time from PO receipt to ex-factory date for your buyers (does it differ between Talbots, Tommy Bahama, etc.)?
7. Could I see a sample (blank or buyer-redacted) TNA template and PO format?
8. What triggers a TNA date to be revised, and what's the escalation process when a date slips?

**Buyer communication**
9. How is communication with overseas buyers structured (email cadence, weekly calls, buyer's India liaison office)?
10. What information does a merchandiser need to report to the buyer at each milestone (lab dip approval, fit approval, PP sample, etc.)?
11. What are the most common reasons for delays or buyer escalations, and how are they handled?

**Costing & approvals**
12. How is initial costing/quotation done — who's involved (merchandiser, costing team, sourcing)?
13. What approvals are needed before bulk fabric/trim booking (lab dip, strike-off, fit approval, etc.)?
14. How does the merchandiser coordinate between sampling, production, and QA to keep the order on track?

**Tools**
15. What system/software is used for order tracking — ERP, Excel, or buyer-specific portals (e.g. a Talbots vendor portal)?
16. What reports does a merchandiser generate weekly/monthly for management?

## 3. Sampling Department
*Ask: Sampling Manager / Pattern Master*

1. What types of samples are developed here (proto, fit, PP, size set, salesman/shipment sample, photo sample)?
2. What's the sampling department's hierarchy and headcount?
3. What's the process flow from tech pack receipt to sample dispatch?
4. How long does each sample type typically take to develop and get buyer approval?
5. What software is used for pattern making (TUKAcad, Optitex, Gerber)?
6. What machines are on the sampling floor, and how many sampling lines/tailors are there?
7. How is fabric consumption/yield estimated at the sampling stage, before bulk marker is made?
8. What happens when a buyer rejects a sample — what's the revision and re-submission process?
9. How is the sample room organized for traceability of fit comments and buyer feedback (style + comment history)?

---

## 4. Sourcing / Raw Material Department (Fabric & Trims)
*Ask: Sourcing Manager / Fabric Procurement Head*

1. What's the department's structure — separate fabric and trims teams, or combined?
2. Who are the key fabric/trim vendors, and how are they approved/onboarded (vendor compliance audits)?
3. What's the process flow from fabric requirement (from costing/BOM) to fabric in-house?
4. How is fabric quality checked on receipt — what's the inspection/4-point system used?
5. What's the rejection/return procedure when a fabric lot fails inspection?
6. How is inventory of fabric and trims tracked and reconciled against consumption (any wastage %)?
7. What lead times are typical for fabric procurement — domestic vs. imported?
8. How does sourcing coordinate with merchandising on lab dip and bulk fabric approval cycles?

---

## 5. Production Planning Department
*Ask: Planning Manager / PPC Head*

1. What's the planning process flow — how does an approved order become a line-loading plan?
2. How is line capacity calculated and allocated across multiple buyers/styles running concurrently?
3. What's the typical line efficiency/SAM (standard allotted minute) used in planning?
4. How are bottlenecks (e.g. fabric delay, sample approval delay) absorbed into the plan — is there buffer built in?
5. What system is used for capacity planning (ERP module, Excel, Gantt-based tool)?
6. How far in advance is the production plan locked, and how often is it revised?
7. Who has authority to push/swap a line if a higher-priority order comes in?

---

## 6. Cutting Department
*Ask: Cutting Manager*

1. What's the department structure, and how many cutting tables/lines does the unit have?
2. What CAD software is used for marker planning, and who creates the marker (in-house pattern team or merchandising-linked CAD room)?
3. What's the process flow from fabric receipt to cut panels reaching the sewing line?
4. Is laying manual, semi-automatic, or fully automated? What machines are used?
5. How is fabric relaxation handled before cutting (especially for knits)?
6. What's the bundling and ticketing system used to track cut panels through production?
7. What is CPI (Cut Panel Inspection) here, and at what rejection rate does a lay get flagged?
8. Is PAD printing or any pre-sewing marking/printing done at this stage?
9. What's the average fabric utilization % achieved, and how is wastage tracked?

---

## 7. Production (Sewing) Department
*Ask: Production Manager / Floor-in-Charge*

1. What's the department hierarchy and floor headcount (operators, supervisors, line leaders)?
2. How many production lines are there, and what's the average line capacity (pieces/day)?
3. What's the pre-production process before a new style starts on the line (machine layout, operator briefing, trial run)?
4. What machines/specialized equipment are used (and any buyer-specific machine requirements, e.g. for Tommy Bahama's woven shirts)?
5. What in-line quality checkpoints exist on the floor, and how are quality workstations integrated into the line?
6. How is daily/hourly production tracked — manual boards, digital production control board, andon system?
7. What lean tools (5S, Kaizen, line balancing) are used on the floor?
8. What's a typical line efficiency % and how is it calculated/reported?
9. How are defects identified on the floor handled — rework loop, spot cleaning?

## 8. Industrial Engineering Department *(ask only if it exists as a distinct function)*
*Ask: IE Manager / Work Study Officer*

1. What's the department's structure and headcount?
2. How are Standard Operation/Time studies conducted for a new style?
3. What's the process for line balancing and skill matrix mapping of operators?
4. How is capacity study done, and how does it feed into planning's line-loading decisions?
5. Is Yamazumi or any similar load-balancing chart used? Could I see a sample?
6. What continuous improvement initiatives has IE led recently?
7. How are operator incentives linked to output/efficiency, if at all?

---

## 9. Quality Assurance Department
*Ask: QA Manager / QC Head*

1. What's the QA hierarchy — in-line QC, end-line QC, final audit — and how do they differ in role?
2. What AQL (Acceptable Quality Limit) standard is followed, and does it vary by buyer?
3. What's the in-line inspection checkpoint frequency on the sewing floor?
4. What's the end-line/final inspection process before garments go to finishing?
5. How are needle detection and metal detection handled — at what stage, and what's the procedure on a failed scan?
6. What's the defect classification system used (critical/major/minor), and what are the most common recurring defects?
7. Is there a Central Inspection Facility or equivalent, and how does a carton/lot get cleared through it?
8. How are buyer QA audits (e.g. a Talbots or Johnny Was quality auditor visit) prepared for and conducted?
9. What documentation is generated per shipment for quality sign-off?

---

## 10. Finishing & Packing / Finished Goods Department
*Ask: Finishing Manager / Packing In-Charge*

1. What's the department structure and headcount?
2. What's the process flow from sewn garment to packed carton (trimming, pressing, folding, tagging, poly-bagging, cartoning)?
3. How is packing done according to buyer-specific packing instructions (ratio, assortment, carton marking)?
4. What's the carton/storage system before dispatch — how are cartons tracked (manual log, barcode, RFID)?
5. What's the final audit process before a shipment is cleared (sample pull %, checkpoints)?
6. How are returned or rejected finished goods handled?
7. What are the major export destinations/ports used for shipments to your buyers?

---

## 11. Compliance Department (Social & Technical)
*Ask: Compliance Manager — this is often overlooked but is highly relevant for export houses*

1. What compliance certifications/standards does the unit follow (WRAP, SEDEX/SMETA, buyer-specific COCs)?
2. How often are buyer compliance audits conducted, and what's a typical audit checklist covering?
3. What's the corrective action process (CAP) when a non-compliance is flagged?
4. How is fire safety, chemical management (ZDHC), and worker welfare monitored?
5. How does compliance coordinate with HR and Admin on statutory requirements (PF, ESI, minimum wage, working hours)?
6. What sustainability initiatives (water, energy, waste) is the unit currently running?

---

## 12. Logistics, Shipping & Documentation
*Ask: Logistics/Shipping Coordinator — often sits within or close to Merchandising*

1. What's the process from "goods ready" to "container loaded and shipped"?
2. What documentation does the team prepare per shipment (commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, certificate of origin)?
3. Which freight forwarders/CHA (customs house agents) does RGE work with?
4. How are shipment delays communicated back to merchandising and the buyer?
5. What incoterms are typically used with your buyers (FOB, CIF, etc.)?
6. How is partial shipment vs. full shipment decided when production runs behind?

## 13. Finance & Costing Department
*Ask: Finance Manager / Costing Head*

1. What's the department structure, and how does it interface with merchandising on order costing?
2. How is a garment costed — what are the major cost heads (fabric, trims, CM, overheads, margin)?
3. How are overheads (admin, finance, SG&A) allocated to individual orders/styles?
4. What's the process for currency/exchange rate risk management on export orders?
5. How is profitability tracked per buyer/style, and who reviews it?
6. What internal or buyer-driven financial audits does the unit go through?

## 14. Human Resources & Admin Department
*Ask: HR Manager / Admin Head*

1. What's the HR department's structure and key functions (recruitment, T&D, payroll, welfare, employee engagement)?
2. What's the recruitment process for shop-floor workers vs. staff/merchandising roles?
3. What training programs exist for new operators and for merchandising trainees/interns?
4. How is attendance and retention tracked, and what's the current attrition rate roughly?
5. What welfare facilities exist (canteen, transport, creche) and how are they resourced?
6. What's the salary advancement/appraisal cycle for staff?

## 15. IT / ERP Systems
*Ask: IT Head / ERP Administrator*

1. What ERP system does RGE use (SAP, custom-built, or a specific apparel ERP)?
2. Which departments are integrated into the ERP, and which still rely on Excel/manual processes?
3. How does the ERP handle TNA tracking, order status, and inventory — and where are the gaps?
4. Is there any buyer-facing portal integration (e.g. vendor compliance portals for Talbots/Tommy Bahama)?
5. What's IT support structure like across shifts?

## 16. Improvement Project (your "Project 1/2" chapters)

Once you've done the rounds above, look for a real, current operational gap to propose a solution for — this is what made your senior's report stand out (their dashboard and FG process proposals came from genuine floor problems, not invented ones). Good prompting questions to ask any department head:

1. "What's one recurring problem in your department that eats up the most time or causes the most rework?"
2. "If you could fix one manual/paper-based process here, what would it be?"
3. "Where do you lose the most visibility/traceability — on the floor, in tracking, or in buyer communication?"

Given you're already deep into building TrackTale GM (your TNA/buyer-communication tracker), there's a natural, genuinely authentic angle here: you could frame one of your two projects around a real TNA/merchandising tracking gap you observe at RGE — that would also double as validation for your own tool's logic.

## Suggested Order of Interviews

1. Industry mentor / Merchandising Manager (sets context, points you to the right people)
2. Merchandising team (deepest section — multiple sittings)
3. Sampling → Sourcing → Planning → Cutting → Production → QA → Finishing (follow the actual order flow — easiest to understand sequentially)
4. Compliance, Logistics, Finance (cross-functional, can be done anytime)
5. HR, Admin, IT (usually quick, can be clustered toward the end)
6. Revisit merchandising once you've seen the whole flow — you'll have much sharper follow-up questions by then
